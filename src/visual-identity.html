Normality vs Authenticity

The task of developing our own visual language comes down to finding a balance between identification in the existing competitive landscape and creating a memorable authentic image.

Normality means Modulate:

  • looks like a tech company,
  • looks like an AI company,
  • looks like it‘s 2026

Can be achieved through “normalization” relative to key players.

Authenticity means Modulate:

  • looks like Modulate,
  • feels modulatish,
  • gives Modulate vibes

Can be achieved through a directed search through completely random images and an attempt to see ourselves in them. At the same time, it's useful to be peripherally aware of existing artifacts and remember brand values.

Good examples of normality.

Characteristics of normality:

  • the primary color is black or dark
  • the graphics are very simple
  • for wordmarks, low-contrast closed grotesques are used (a long-standing trend of the 2020s), a pleasant exception — Elevenlabs, a grotesque with increased contrast

There has to be something square

The logo construction should be: something square + wordmark. If we don't have a recognizable brand element with square proportions, we'll have to invent it every time we create a social media account or an app icon for any platform.

The logo doesn't provide an answer to the question "what will we have as an icon", we have to invent something each time, and this doesn't work for brand recognition and consistency.

The logo is text-based, a special symbol that isn't part of the logo is used for the icon. (OpenAI's guidelines say that Blossom cannot be used as primary branding, but in fact this is their main identifier.)

The logo has a square symbol and it's used when a square icon is needed.
